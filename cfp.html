Call For Papers

Submission Requirements

We invite contributions that explore the incorporation of scientific knowledge in GenAI methodologies for diverse applications. The workshop aims to bring together researchers from diverse backgrounds to share insights, methodologies, and applications in GenAI for Science. We welcome papers on a broad range of topics, including but not limited to:

  • Knowledge-Guided Loss Functions: Incorporating scientific principles and constraints directly into objective functions.
  • Knowledge-guided Pretraining: Leveraging domain knowledge to enhance pretraining strategies for scientific applications.
  • Knowledge-guided Post-training: Fine-tuning models with scientific knowledge to improve performance on downstream tasks.
  • Knowledge-guided Dataset Selection: Using domain expertise to curate and select relevant training data.
  • Knowledge-guided GenAI Architectures: Designing neural network architectures that embed scientific knowledge and inductive biases.
  • Knowledge-guided Interpretable AI: Developing models that provide scientifically meaningful explanations and insights.
  • Knowledge-guided Reinforcement Learning: Integrating domain knowledge into RL frameworks for scientific decision-making.
  • Hybrid-Process-GenAI Models: Combining mechanistic models with data-driven approaches for improved predictions.
  • Scientific Foundation Models: Large-scale models pretrained on scientific data for broad applicability.
  • LLMs and multi-modal LLMs for Science: Leveraging language and vision models for scientific discovery and reasoning.
  • Forward and Inverse modeling: Using GenAI for predicting outcomes and inferring parameters from observations.
  • Datasets and Benchmarks: Creating standardized datasets and evaluation metrics for scientific GenAI tasks.

We encourage submissions that cater to a broad set of use-cases in science and engineering, such as physics, chemistry, biology, drug design, material science, protein design and folding, hydrology, aquatic sciences, climate/weather, evolution, biodiversity, agriculture, solving PDEs, virology, and epidemiology.

Submission Guidelines

We invite submissions including:

  • Short papers presenting ongoing research or work submitted to other venues (up to 5 pages, excluding references)
  • Full papers that have been published at a peer-reviewed venue after January 1st, 2026 (up to 9 pages, excluding references)

All the submissions must be anonymous and follow the ICML style.

An appendix (without page limits) is allowed after references and should be submitted in the same file as the main text.

The full paper submissions must not violate any dual-submission or anonymity policies of the accepted venues.

The accepted papers will NOT be archived in ICML proceedings. This allows the authors to extend their work afterward and submit it to other venues.
